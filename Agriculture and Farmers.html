Agriculture and Farmers Schemes

1. PM-KISAN (Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi)
Objective: Provide assured income support to farmers.
Eligibility: All landholding farmer families (excluding income tax payers, institutional landholders, etc.).
Benefits:
₹6,000 per year, paid in three instalments of ₹2,000 each via Direct Benefit Transfer.
Mandatory eKYC for beneficiaries.
Implementation: Launched in 2019; farmers register via PM-KISAN portal or CSCs.

2. Kisan Credit Card (KCC)
Objective: Provide timely and affordable credit to farmers.
Eligibility: Farmers engaged in crop cultivation, allied activities, and fisheries.
Benefits:
Revolving cash credit facility.
Interest rate ~7% p.a., reduced to 4% with government subvention and prompt repayment incentive.
Loan limit based on landholding and cropping pattern.
Implementation: Apply via banks (SBI, NABARD-linked institutions).

3. Soil Health Card Scheme
Objective: Promote balanced fertilizer use and improve soil productivity.
Eligibility: All farmers.
Benefits:
Free soil testing and nutrient analysis.
Recommendations for fertilizer and organic inputs.
Over 25 crore cards distributed; 8,272 soil testing labs established nationwide.
Implementation: Ministry of Agriculture; cards generated via SHC portal.

4. RKVY (Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana – Raftaar)
Objective: Accelerate agricultural growth through infrastructure, innovation, and state-led planning.
Eligibility: State governments propose projects; farmers benefit indirectly.
Benefits:
Grants for modernization, entrepreneurship, and agri-startups.
Focus on food security, rural development, and sustainable farming.
Implementation: Centrally sponsored scheme; decentralized planning with State Level Sanctioning Committees.

5. NMSA (National Mission on Sustainable Agriculture)
Objective: Promote climate-resilient farming practices.
Eligibility: Farmers in rainfed and climate-vulnerable areas.
Benefits:
Integrated farming systems.
Efficient water management (drip/sprinkler irrigation).
Soil health management and resource conservation.
Implementation: Launched in 2014–15 under National Action Plan on Climate Change.

6. PMFBY (Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana)
Objective: Provide affordable crop insurance against natural calamities.
Eligibility: All farmers (voluntary since Kharif 2020).
Benefits:
Covers risks from pre-sowing to post-harvest.
Premium: 2% for Kharif crops, 1.5% for Rabi crops, 5% for horticulture.
Compensation for yield losses due to drought, flood, pests, etc.
Implementation: Launched in 2016; implemented via insurance companies, banks, and CSCs.

7. e-NAM (National Agriculture Market)
Objective: Create a unified national market for agricultural commodities.
Eligibility: Farmers registered with APMC mandis.
Benefits:
Online trading platform for transparent price discovery.
Eliminates middlemen; farmers can sell nationwide.
Over 1,400 mandis integrated.
Implementation: Launched in 2016; implemented by SFAC under Ministry of Agriculture.

8. PKVY (Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana)
Objective: Promote organic farming and soil health.
Eligibility: Farmers organized into clusters.
Benefits:
Financial support for organic inputs and certification.
15 lakh hectares under organic farming; 25 lakh farmers benefitted.
Implementation: Launched in 2015 under NMSA; linked with Jaivik Kheti portal.

9. Krishi Bhagya (Karnataka)
Objective: Support dryland farmers with water conservation.
Eligibility: Farmers in rainfed areas of Karnataka.
Benefits:
Subsidy (up to 90%) for farm ponds, drip irrigation, diesel pumps, tarpaulin covers.
Focus on rainwater harvesting for sustainable agriculture.
Implementation: State government scheme; extended to all taluks.

10. Raitha Siri (Karnataka)
Objective: Promote millet cultivation and support farmers financially.
Eligibility: Millet farmers in Karnataka.
Benefits:
Financial assistance up to ₹10,000 per hectare.
Encourages climate-resilient crops and better farming methods.
Implementation: State-sponsored scheme; applications via agriculture department portals.
