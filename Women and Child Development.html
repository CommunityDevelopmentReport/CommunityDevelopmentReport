Women and Child Development Schemes

1. ICDS (Integrated Child Development Services)
Objective: Provide nutrition, health, and preschool education to children under 6 years and support to mothers.
Eligibility: Children (0–6 years), pregnant women, lactating mothers.
Benefits:
Supplementary nutrition, immunization, health check-ups.
Preschool education via Anganwadi centres.
Nutrition and health counseling for mothers.
Implementation: Launched in 1975; one of the world’s largest community outreach programs.

2. Mahila Shakti Kendra
Objective: Empower rural women through community participation.
Eligibility: Women in rural areas, especially marginalized groups.
Benefits:
Skill development, digital literacy, and health awareness.
Support for entrepreneurship and self-help groups.
District-level resource centers for women.
Implementation: Launched in 2017–18; implemented through Anganwadi and block-level centers.

3. Working Women Hostel Scheme
Objective: Provide safe and affordable accommodation for working women.
Eligibility: Working women (including single women, widows, divorced women) with income below a prescribed limit.
Benefits:
Subsidized hostel facilities with daycare support for children.
Security and basic amenities.
Encourages women’s participation in workforce.
Implementation: Ministry of Women & Child Development; NGOs and institutions run hostels with government grants.

4. National Creche Scheme
Objective: Provide daycare facilities for children of working mothers.
Eligibility: Children (6 months–6 years) of working women from low-income groups.
Benefits:
Daycare, nutrition, early childhood education.
Safe environment for children while mothers work.
Reduces dropout rates among working women.
Implementation: Centrally sponsored scheme; run by NGOs with government support.

5. Stand-Up India
Objective: Promote entrepreneurship among women and SC/ST communities.
Eligibility: Women entrepreneurs and SC/ST individuals starting new enterprises.
Benefits:
Bank loans between ₹10 lakh–₹1 crore for setting up enterprises.
Handholding support for project planning and execution.
Encourages job creation and financial independence.
Implementation: Launched in 2016; implemented via scheduled commercial banks.
