Education and Skill Development Schemes

1. Samagra Shiksha Abhiyan
Objective: Integrate school education from pre-primary to Class 12.
Eligibility: All government and aided schools.
Benefits:
Funds for infrastructure, teacher training, ICT-enabled classrooms.
Special focus on inclusive education for children with disabilities.
Bridges gaps between elementary, secondary, and senior secondary education.
Implementation: Launched in 2018; merged SSA, RMSA, and Teacher Education schemes.

2. Mid-Day Meal Scheme
Improve nutritional status of school children and encourage attendance.
Eligibility: Students of government and aided primary and upper primary schools.
Benefits:
Free cooked meals during school hours.
Nutritional norms: 450 calories (primary), 700 calories (upper primary).
Reduces dropout rates and supports child development.
Implementation: Launched in 1995; world’s largest school feeding program.

3. Beti Bachao Beti Padhao (BBBP)
Objective: Promote survival, protection, and education of the girl child.
Eligibility: All districts with low Child Sex Ratio (CSR).
Benefits:
Awareness campaigns against gender bias.
Scholarships and incentives for girl education.
Convergence of ministries: Women & Child Development, Health, Education.
Implementation: Launched in 2015; expanded nationwide.

National Digital Literacy Mission (NDLM)
Objective: Make at least one person per household digitally literate.
Eligibility: Citizens aged 14–60 years, especially rural households.
Benefits:
Free training in basic digital skills (email, online payments, internet use).
Certification after training.
Promotes digital inclusion.
Implementation: Launched in 2014; implemented via CSCs and NGOs.

5. Skill India Mission
Objective: Provide vocational training to youth for employability.
Eligibility: Youth aged 15–59 years.
Benefits:
Training in 40+ sectors (construction, IT, healthcare, etc.).
Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL).
Placement support and entrepreneurship encouragement.
Implementation: Launched in 2015; umbrella mission covering PMKVY, DDU-GKY, etc.

6. EMRS (Eklavya Model Residential Schools)
Objective: Provide quality education to tribal children.
Eligibility: Scheduled Tribe students in rural/tribal areas.
Benefits:
Free residential schooling from Class 6–12.
Focus on STEM, sports, and cultural education.
Target: one EMRS in every block with >50% ST population.
Implementation: Ministry of Tribal Affairs; over 400 schools sanctioned.

7. Vidya Siri (Karnataka)
Objective: Support students from economically weaker sections with hostel facilities.
Eligibility: SC/ST/OBC students studying in higher education institutions.
Benefits:
Free hostel accommodation and food.
Reduces dropout rates among rural and marginalized students.
Implementation: State government scheme under Department of Backward Classes Welfare.

8. Prerana Scheme (Karnataka)
Objective: Provide scholarships to SC/ST students for higher education.
Eligibility: SC/ST students pursuing professional courses (engineering, medicine, etc.).
Benefits:
Financial assistance for tuition fees, books, and living expenses.
Encourages participation in competitive exams and higher studies.
Implementation: State government initiative; applications via ePass Karnataka portal.

9. National Scholarship Portal (NSP)
Objective: Centralized platform for applying to multiple scholarships.
Eligibility: Students from SC, ST, OBC, minority, and economically weaker sections.
Benefits:
Transparent application and disbursement process.
Covers pre-matric, post-matric, merit-based, and professional course scholarships.
Direct Benefit Transfer to student accounts.
Implementation: Launched in 2016; managed by Ministry of Electronics & IT.

10. PM e-Vidya
Objective: Provide digital learning resources to students during COVID-19 and beyond.
Eligibility: All school students nationwide.
Benefits:
One dedicated TV channel per class (Class 1–12).
Digital platforms like DIKSHA, SWAYAM, and ePathshala.
Focus on bridging digital divide in rural areas.
Implementation: Launched in 2020 under Atmanirbhar Bharat package.
