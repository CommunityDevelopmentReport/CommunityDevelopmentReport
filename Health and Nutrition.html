Health and Nutrition Schemes

1. Ayushman Bharat – PM-JAY (Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana)
Objective: Provide free health insurance to poor and vulnerable families.
Eligibility: Families identified in SECC 2011 database; no cap on family size or age.
Benefits:
₹5 lakh per family per year for secondary and tertiary care hospitalization.
Cashless treatment at empaneled hospitals.
Covers 1,350+ medical packages.
Implementation: Launched in 2018; managed by National Health Authority.

2. JSY (Janani Suraksha Yojana)
Objective: Promote institutional deliveries to reduce maternal and infant mortality.
Eligibility: Pregnant women from Below Poverty Line (BPL) households.
Benefits:
Cash incentive for delivering in government or accredited private hospitals.
ASHA workers receive incentives for facilitating institutional deliveries.
Implementation: Launched in 2005 under NHM.

3. NHM (National Health Mission)
Objective: Strengthen rural and urban health infrastructure.
Eligibility: Entire population, with focus on rural poor.
Benefits:
Free medicines, diagnostics, and maternal-child health services.
Establishment of Health & Wellness Centres.
Training of healthcare workers.
Implementation: Umbrella program launched in 2013, merging NRHM and NUHM.

4. Poshan Abhiyaan (National Nutrition Mission)
Objective: Reduce malnutrition among children, pregnant women, and lactating mothers.
Eligibility: Women and children under ICDS framework.
Benefits:
Growth monitoring, nutrition counseling, and fortified food distribution.
Target to reduce stunting, anemia, and low birth weight.
Use of technology (Poshan Tracker app).
Implementation: Launched in 2018; convergence of multiple ministries.

5. PMMVY (Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana)
Objective: Provide maternity benefits to ensure safe delivery and nutrition.
Eligibility: Pregnant women and lactating mothers (first child only).
Benefits:
₹5,000 in three instalments for antenatal care, institutional delivery, and postnatal care.
Linked with Janani Suraksha Yojana for additional benefits.
Implementation: Launched in 2017 under Ministry of Women & Child Development.

6. Mission Indradhanush
Objective: Achieve universal immunization coverage for children and pregnant women.
Eligibility: Children up to 2 years and pregnant women.
Benefits:
Free vaccination against 12 vaccine-preventable diseases (e.g., polio, measles, hepatitis B).
Intensified campaigns in high-focus districts.
Implementation: Launched in 2014; expanded as Intensified Mission Indradhanush.

7. Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram (RBSK)
Objective: Early identification and treatment of health conditions in children.
Eligibility: Children aged 0–18 years (Anganwadi, schools).
Benefits:
Screening for 4Ds: Defects at birth, Diseases, Deficiencies, Developmental delays.
Free treatment and referral support.
Implementation: Launched under NHM; mobile health teams visit schools and Anganwadis.

8. Jan Aushadhi Scheme(Pradhan Mantri Bhartiya Janaushadhi Pariyojana)
Objective: Provide affordable generic medicines to all.
Eligibility: Open to all citizens.
Benefits:
Medicines at 50–90% lower prices than branded equivalents.
Over 10,000 Jan Aushadhi Kendras across India.
Implementation: Managed by BPPI under Department of Pharmaceuticals.

9. Vatsalya Scheme (Karnataka)
Objective: Provide free medical treatment to BPL families.
Eligibility: BPL cardholders in Karnataka.
Benefits:
Free treatment for serious illnesses in government and selected private hospitals.
Coverage for maternal and child health services.
Implementation: State government initiative; linked with Suvarna Arogya Suraksha Trust.

10. Arogya Karnataka
Objective: Universal health coverage for Karnataka residents.
Eligibility: All residents of Karnataka; BPL families get full coverage, APL families partial.
Benefits:
₹5 lakh per family per year for BPL households.
Cashless treatment in empaneled hospitals.
Covers major secondary and tertiary care procedures.
Implementation: Launched in 2018; integrated with Ayushman Bharat for co-funding.
