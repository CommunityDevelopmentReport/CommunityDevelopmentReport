Housing and Infrastructure Schemes

1. PMAY-G (Pradhan Mantri Awaas Yojana – Gramin)
Objective: Provide “Housing for All” by building pucca houses for rural poor.
Eligibility: Households without homes or living in kutcha houses (as per SECC data).
Benefits:
Financial assistance for house construction.
Minimum unit size increased to 25 sq.m. with cooking space.
Implementation: Launched in 2016; target of 2.95 crore houses by 2022.

2. PMGSY (Pradhan Mantri Gram Sadak Yojana)
Objective: Provide all-weather road connectivity to unconnected rural habitations.
Eligibility: Villages with population >500 (plains) and >250 (hilly/tribal areas).
Benefits:
Improved access to markets, schools, and healthcare.
Boosts rural economy and employment.
Implementation: Launched in 2000; over 7.8 lakh km of roads completed.

3. Jal Jeevan Mission
Objective: Provide piped tap water to every rural household by 2024.
Eligibility: All rural households.
Benefits:
55 litres per capita per day of safe drinking water.
Focus on water conservation, greywater reuse, and rainwater harvesting.
Implementation: Community-led approach under Ministry of Jal Shakti.

4. Saubhagya Scheme(Pradhan Mantri Sahaj Bijli Har Ghar Yojana)
Objective: Universal household electrification.
Eligibility: Households identified via SECC 2011; others can pay ₹500 for connection.
Benefits:
Free electricity connections for poor households.
Reduced reliance on kerosene lamps.
Implementation: Launched in 2017; REC is nodal agency.

5. Smart Village Program (Karnataka)
Objective: ICT-enabled holistic village development.
Eligibility: Implemented in selected villages of Karnataka.
Benefits:
Digital governance, smart infrastructure, renewable energy.
Improved education, healthcare, and connectivity.
Implementation: State-led initiative aligned with Smart City mission.
