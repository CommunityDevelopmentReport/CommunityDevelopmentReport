Social Security and Welfare Schemes

1. Old Age Pension (IGNOAPS – Indira Gandhi National Old Age Pension Scheme)
Objective: Provide financial support to elderly citizens.
Eligibility: BPL households; individuals aged 60+ years.
Benefits:
₹200 per month for ages 60–79.
₹500 per month for ages 80+.
States often add top-up amounts.
Implementation: Part of NSAP; funds transferred via DBT.

2. Widow Pension (Indira Gandhi National Widow Pension Scheme – IGNWPS)
Objective: Universalize secondary education (9–12 years).
Objective: Support widowed women financially.
Eligibility: Widows aged 40–59 years from BPL households.
Benefits:
₹300 per month (minimum); states may add more.
Helps with basic sustenance.
Implementation: Under NSAP; disbursed through state social welfare departments.

3. Disability Pension (Indira Gandhi National Disability Pension Scheme – IGNDPS)
Objective: Provide financial aid to persons with severe disabilities.
Eligibility: BPL households; persons aged 18–79 with ≥80% disability.
Benefits:
₹300 per month (minimum); states may enhance.
Support for livelihood and medical needs.
Implementation: Under NSAP; DBT transfer.

4. NSAP (National Social Assistance Programme)
Objective: Umbrella program for social pensions and assistance.
Eligibility: BPL households (elderly, widows, disabled).
Benefits:
Covers IGNOAPS, IGNWPS, IGNDPS.
Provides unconditional cash transfers for vulnerable groups.
Implementation: Launched in 1995; centrally sponsored scheme.

5. Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana
Objective: Encourage savings for the education and marriage of girl children.
Eligibility: Parents/guardians of girl children below 10 years.
Benefits:
Small deposit scheme with high interest (~8% p.a.).
Tax benefits under Section 80C.
Account matures after 21 years or marriage of girl after 18.
Implementation: Launched in 2015 under Beti Bachao Beti Padhao; accounts opened in banks/post offices.

6. Atal Pension Yojana (APY)
Objective: Provide pension to unorganized sector workers.
Eligibility: Citizens aged 18–40 years with bank account.
Benefits:
Guaranteed pension of ₹1,000–₹5,000 per month after age 60.
Government co-contribution for eligible subscribers.
Auto-debit facility for contributions.
Implementation: Launched in 2015; managed by PFRDA.

7. PMJJBY (Pradhan Mantri Jeevan Jyoti Bima Yojana)
Objective: Provide affordable life insurance.
Eligibility: Citizens aged 18–50 years with bank account.
Benefits:
₹2 lakh life insurance cover.
Premium: ₹436 per year.
Renewable annually.
Implementation: Launched in 2015; implemented via banks and insurance companies.

8. PMSBY (Pradhan Mantri Suraksha Bima Yojana)
Objective: Provide accident insurance.
Eligibility: Citizens aged 18–70 years with bank account.
Benefits:
₹2 lakh cover for accidental death or full disability.
₹1 lakh cover for partial disability.
Premium: ₹20 per year.
Implementation: Launched in 2015; implemented via banks and insurance companies.

9. Bhagyalakshmi Scheme (Karnataka)
Objective: Promote welfare of girl children in BPL families.
Eligibility: Girl children born in BPL families in Karnataka.
Benefits:
Financial assistance deposited in the child’s name.
Maturity benefits when the girl turns 18 (if unmarried).
Encourages education and discourages child marriage.
Implementation: State government scheme; managed by Women & Child Development Department.

10. Sandhya Suraksha (Karnataka)
Objective: Provide pension to senior citizens in Karnataka.
Eligibility: Residents aged 60+ years from BPL families.
Benefits:
Monthly pension (₹400–₹1,000 depending on category).
Additional support for medical care and sustenance.
Implementation: State government initiative; disbursed via social welfare department.
