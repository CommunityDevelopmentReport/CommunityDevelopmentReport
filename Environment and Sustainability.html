Environment and Sustainability Schemes

1. Swachh Bharat Mission (Gramin)
Objective: Achieve universal sanitation coverage in rural India.
Eligibility: All rural households.
Benefits:
Financial assistance for toilet construction.
Awareness campaigns for hygiene and cleanliness.
Declared India “Open Defecation Free” in 2019.
Implementation: Launched in 2014; Ministry of Jal Shakti oversees rural component.

2. National Afforestation Programme
Objective: Increase forest cover and restore degraded ecosystems.
Eligibility: Forest fringe communities, NGOs, and state forest departments.
Benefits:
Grants for tree plantation drives.
Eco-restoration of degraded forests.
Promotes biodiversity and climate resilience.
Implementation: Ministry of Environment, Forest & Climate Change; decentralized via Forest Development Agencies.

3. Renewable Energy Development Schemes
Objective: Promote renewable energy adoption in rural and urban areas.
Eligibility: Farmers, households, and institutions.
Benefits:
Subsidies for solar pumps, biogas plants, and small wind systems.
Reduces dependence on fossil fuels.
Encourages sustainable energy use.
Implementation: Ministry of New & Renewable Energy (MNRE); linked with KUSUM scheme for solar pumps.

4. PM Ujjwala Yojana
Objective: Provide clean cooking fuel to poor households.
Eligibility: Women from BPL households with SECC data or ration card.
Benefits:
Free LPG connection with initial refill and stove.
Reduces indoor air pollution and health risks.
Empowerment of women through clean energy access.
Implementation: Launched in 2016; implemented by Ministry of Petroleum & Natural Gas.

5. Solar Rooftop Subsidy (Karnataka)
Objective: Promote renewable energy adoption through rooftop solar systems.
Eligibility: Households, institutions, and industries in Karnataka.
Benefits:
Subsidy up to 40% for residential rooftop solar installations.
Reduces electricity bills and promotes clean energy.
Net metering allows selling surplus power to grid.
Implementation: State government scheme under Karnataka Renewable Energy Development Ltd. (KREDL).
